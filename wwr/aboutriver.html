About Grijalva

The Grijalva River is an international river shared between Guatemala and Mexico. It is born at more than 4,000 meters above sea level and flows through the American continent until it reaches the Atlantic Ocean.

It's basin drains an area of 22,403 sq miles, of which 10% is in Guatemala and the remaining 90% belongs to Mexico. Its flow of 47,740 million cubic yards per year makes it the largest producer of hydroelectric power in Mexico.

Precipitation

The riverbed is located at the foot of the Sierra de los Cuchumatanes, the Sierra Madre de Chiapas and the Sierra Norte in Mesoamerica. It's geographical location in the depression formed by the elevation of the mountains, right where the cold front from the north meets the tropical air masses, make the Grijalva basin one of the regions with the highest rate of rainfall worldwide.

It has the highest rate of rainfall in the country, with an annual average that exceeds 83 inches, reaching more than twice the national average. The rainfall rate in the basin varies considerably as the river moves toward its mouth.

It runs 373 miles from its source in the Guatemalan mountains to its mouth in the Gulf of Mexico.

Tuxtla Gutierrez

This city is the capital and the most important population center of the Mexican state of Chiapas. In 2015 it had 598,710 inhabitants and is considered one of the best cities to live in Mexico.

The Grijalva River is located 3 miles from the city at a height of 390 meters above sea level, in an area protected by the Cañón del Sumidero National Park. It is located on a geological fault in the Sierra Norte de Chiapas, the canyon walls exceed 1,000 meters above the river level and has a depth of up to 273 yards.

The canyon flows into the artificial lake of the Manuel Moreno Torres dam and is enabled for ecotourism enjoyment with boat rides and kayaks that allow you to admire the fauna and flora of the jungle that occupies the banks. Land alternatives are also offered to explore the routes on foot or by bicycle, carry out climbing, rappelling and zip-lining activities. In addition, you can enjoy the panoramic views from the viewpoints.
